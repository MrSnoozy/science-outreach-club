Students on the Beamline (SotB)

Experiential particle accelerator research at the Canadian Light Source in Saskatoon, SK.

About the Team & SotB Initiative

Our subteam within the Science Outreach Club is participating in the national Students on the Beamline (SotB) program. This immersive program matches high school science cohorts with dedicated runtime at Canada's national synchrotron facility, bridging the gap between high school laboratory fundamentals and large-scale particle physics environments.

Our team consists of advanced physics and chemistry students collaborating on high-temperature thermal transformation research, environmental safety modeling, and analytical spectroscopy design.

General Knowledge: Chlorine Pyrolysis Dynamics

When engineered building materials (like MDF fiberboards or laminate woods with polymer coatings) are thermally recycled through pyrolysis, tracking toxic elements like chlorine is a critical environmental hurdle. Unlike pure organic compounds, treated woods contain industrial resins, flame retardants, or PVC materials that release corrosive gases when heated.

The Core Structural Question

Our project investigates whether chlorine atoms remain locked within the solid biochar matrix as safe, stable mineral deposits (like Sodium Chloride or Potassium Chloride) or assemble into volatile, carcinogenic aromatic structures. Understanding this coordination chemistry allows engineers to optimize cleaner waste-to-energy infrastructures.

Experimental Procedure & Laboratory Controls

To prepare clean samples for our synchrotron run, our team executes a controlled, multi-stage inert thermal decomposition process within our school laboratory tube furnace:

  • 1. Atmospheric Purging

    Samples are isolated inside an air-tight ceramic boat within the furnace tube. A continuous Nitrogen ($N_2$) flow profile at 1–2 L/min is established for several minutes to displace all oxygen, preventing standard combustion from occurring during heating.

  • 2. Moderate Thermal Ramping

    The furnace temperature program is activated, increasing at a steady rate of ~10°C/min until it peaks at a 500°C dwell window. Maintaining this moderate limit ensures cellulose matrices decompose while preventing excessive mineral volatilization.

  • 3. Volatile Acid Mitigation (The Scrubber)

    As gases are driven off, they are swept downstream into an aqueous gas-washing system containing a 2% Sodium Hydroxide ($NaOH$) solution. This alkaline trap safely captures and neutralizes volatile Hydrogen Chloride ($HCl$) emissions before venting.

  • 4. Synchrotron Sample Extraction

    The resulting brittle, carbon-rich biochar residues are cooled completely under continuous nitrogen purge to block room-air oxidation, extracted, and sealed tightly into testing vials for synchrotron analysis.

  • Primary Target: Engineered Woods & Laminate Composites

Synchrotron Mechanics

Synchrotrons use powerful magnetic arrays to accelerate electrons to nearly the speed of light, producing exceptionally brilliant beams of X-ray energy capable of defining molecular-scale target structures.
