Students on the Beamline (SotB) Hub

Central research directory for our BC forestry residue mineral electronic coupling experiments at the Canadian Light Source.

About the Team & SotB Initiative

Our subteam within the Science Outreach Club is participating in the national Students on the Beamline (SotB) program. This immersive project matches advanced chemistry and physics high school students with dedicated beamtime at Canada's national synchrotron facility, bridging the gap between classroom theory and elite particle physics.

General Knowledge: Carbon-Mineral Electronic Coupling

British Columbia produces a massive amount of forestry residue every year from logging operations. Converting this waste into biochar helps store carbon and improve agricultural soils, but the atomic-level mineral properties of BC conifer biochar remain largely unmapped. Our project investigates how trace metals like iron (Fe) and manganese (Mn) interact with the carbon matrix during thermal processing.

X-Ray Spectroscopy & Reduction Hypothesis

We are testing whether graphitizing carbon acts as a direct electron donor, reducing $\text{Fe}^{3+}$ and $\text{Mn}^{4+}$ down to lower oxidation states ($\text{Fe}^{2+}$ and $\text{Mn}^{2+}$). Using the synchrotron beamline, we will isolate the Iron K-edge (~7.11 keV) and Manganese K-edge (~6.54 keV) thresholds to monitor these electron transfers.
