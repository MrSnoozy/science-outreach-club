Students on the Beamline (SotB)

Experiential particle accelerator research at the Canadian Light Source in Saskatoon, SK.

Our Core Research Focus

Our team has been selected to perform original structural and chemical analysis at the Canadian Light Source synchrotron. We are using state-of-the-art beamline technology to investigate materials at the molecular level, bridging concepts across high-level physics and chemistry.

Current Investigation Overview

Hypothesis & Objective: To investigate the high-temperature partitioning, solid-phase retention, and coordination chemistry of chlorine in engineered wood composites and chlorinated polymer matrices during inert thermal degradation[cite: 1, 2, 4, 125]. We aim to determine whether chlorine is sequestered in the resulting biochar as stable inorganic mineral salts (e.g., NaCl, KCl) or locks into complex, hazardous organochlorine polycyclic aromatic structures[cite: 9, 27, 46].

Methodology: Wood composites, laminates, and treated samples undergo slow pyrolysis at a moderate temperature profile peaking at 500°C under a continuous 1–2 L/min Nitrogen (N₂) purge to eliminate oxidation[cite: 55, 126, 127, 131]. Volatile acid pathways—specifically Hydrogen Chloride (HCl) gas—are safely neutralized downstream through a 2% Sodium Hydroxide (NaOH) alkaline gas-washing scrubber system[cite: 139, 143, 144, 145]. The concentrated, uniform solid char residues will then be transferred to the synchrotron[cite: 105, 162, 171].

The SotB Journey & Field Logs

Tracking our progress from experimental proposal to live data collection.

  • Phase 1: Proposal & Peer Review — Completed

    Drafted and submitted our formal synchrotron beamtime application detailing our target composite matrices (MDF fiberboards with resin additives and polymer overlays) [cite: 56, 57] and environmental mitigation significance. The submission successfully cleared safety controls regarding corrosive volatile gas capture pathways[cite: 140, 159].

  • Phase 2: Sample Preparation & Calibration — In Progress

    Executing triplicate batch runs for each material type in a laboratory tube furnace[cite: 126, 130]. Samples are ramped at a moderate rate of ~10°C/min up to a 500°C dwell window for 30–60 minutes to decompose organic cellulose and hemicellulose matrices[cite: 131, 133]. Chars are cooled entirely under inert N₂ flow to below 100°C to eliminate ambient air oxidation before retrieval[cite: 136, 137].

  • Phase 3: Synchrotron Run (Saskatoon, SK) — Upcoming

    Traveling to the CLS facility at the University of Saskatchewan to position our enriched, uniform solid char matrices under operational vacuum constraints[cite: 108, 109, 171]. We will sweep soft X-ray photon energies across the Chlorine K-edge energy threshold (~2.82 keV) to gather near-edge spectra for linear combination fingerprinting[cite: 29, 31, 34].

SotB Expedition Info

  • Location: Canadian Light Source (CLS), Saskatoon, SK [cite: 108]
  • Facility Base: University of Saskatchewan Campus [cite: 108]
  • Analytical Mode: Cl K-edge XANES Spectroscopy (~2.82 keV) [cite: 29]
  • Atmosphere Type: Continuous Nitrogen Purge (1–2 L/min) [cite: 126, 127]
  • Scrubber Solution: 2% Aqueous NaOH (0.5 M Neutralizer) [cite: 144]

About the Facility

The Canadian Light Source is Canada's premier synchrotron light facility[cite: 108]. It uses intense beams of light to analyze the microscopic structure of materials across disciplines[cite: 108].
