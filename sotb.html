About the Team & SotB Initiative

Our subteam within the Science Outreach Club is participating in the national Students on the Beamline (SotB) program. This immersive project matches advanced chemistry and physics high school students with dedicated beamtime at Canada's national synchrotron facility, bridging the gap between classroom theory and elite particle physics.

General Knowledge: Chlorine Pyrolysis Dynamics

When engineered materials (like MDF fiberboards or laminates with polymer coatings) undergo high-temperature recycling, tracking toxic additives like chlorine is critical. Our project investigates whether chlorine is safely sequestered into the solid biochar matrix as stable mineral salts (NaCl, KCl) or if it locks into complex organochlorine aromatic structures.

X-Ray Spectroscopy Focus

By bringing our uniform char residues to the synchrotron beamline, we will target the Chlorine K-edge absorption threshold (~2.82 keV) to precisely map out the coordination chemistry of our samples.
