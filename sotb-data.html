Data Processing & Spectral Fingerprinting

How we decode molecular structures using X-ray Absorption Near-Edge Structure (XANES).

Understanding the Chlorine K-edge Spectrum

When our biochar samples are placed inside the synchrotron experimental chamber, we shoot soft X-ray light at them, stepping the energy from roughly 2.81 keV up through 2.85 keV. As the energy hits the binding threshold of core Chlorine electrons, a sharp jump in absorption occurs (the "K-edge").

Linear Combination Fitting (LCF) Strategy

Raw curves from the beamline don't automatically state what molecules are present. To figure it out, our team will use software to run Linear Combination Fitting. We mix different percentages of mathematically pristine "Reference Standards" together until they match our experimental biochar curve perfectly.

Our Core Chemical Benchmarks

To differentiate safe mineral salts from dangerous pollutants, our data will be actively weighed against these specific material signatures found in our literature reviews:

Reference Standard Group What it Represents in our Biochar
Inorganic Chlorides (NaCl, KCl, CuCl₂) Indicates chlorine safely stabilized as benign mineral crystals within the wood ash.
Aliphatic Organochlorines (Raw PVC Overlays) Indicates original plastic polymers failed to completely crack or pyrolyze during the furnace run.
Aromatic Organochlorines (Chlorobenzene / Organohalogens) Indicates a hazardous outcome: chlorine chemically bound into stable, toxic carbon-ring structures.
