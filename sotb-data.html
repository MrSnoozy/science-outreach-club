2. XANES Edge Spectroscopy & LCF Modelling

By bringing our processed chars to the synchrotron, we isolate the Iron K-edge (~7.11 keV) and Manganese K-edge (~6.54 keV) thresholds to monitor electronic state configurations. Linear Combination Fitting (LCF) reconstructs these absorption signatures using pristine reference parameters.

3. Environmental Mechanics & Radical Activation Pathways

Understanding the exact oxidation ratios of these metals allows us to safely deploy our chars in water treatment and agricultural contexts. For instance, a high concentration of localized $\text{Fe}^{2+}$ enables the initiation of advanced Fenton radical chain reactions when interacting with peroxides in an aqueous environment:

Fenton Reaction Cascade:

$$\text{Fe}^{2+} + \text{H}_2\text{O}_2 \rightarrow \text{Fe}^{3+} + \bullet\text{OH} + \text{OH}^-$$

$$\text{Fe}^{3+} + \text{H}_2\text{O}_2 \rightarrow \text{Fe}^{2+} + \text{HO}_2\bullet + \text{H}^+$$

By establishing the exact valency distribution via XANES, we can accurately predict how efficiently these radicals will deploy to tear down environmental pollutants.

4. Complementary Structural Data

To fully validate our **carbon-mineral electronic coupling hypothesis**, our XANES oxidation datasets will be actively paired against metrics assessing the structural order of the surrounding carbon skeleton.
