Pyrolysis Mechanics & Safety Constraints

To safely capture clean solid biochars from composite samples without introducing oxygen anomalies, our club implements the following rigorous framework:

  1. Inert Purging: Samples are loaded into a ceramic or stainless boat within the tube furnace. An initial continuous flow of Nitrogen (N₂) at 1–2 L/min runs for several minutes to fully sweep atmospheric oxygen out.
  2. Thermal Progression: The furnace runs an automated program ascending at ~10°C/min up to a final 500°C target window. It holds this boundary for 30–60 minutes to eliminate volatile organic matrices completely.
  3. Downstream Effluent Capture: Corrosive volatile fractions—specifically Hydrogen Chloride (HCl) gas—are forced down-tube through a safe, aqueous gas-washing system loaded with a 2% Sodium Hydroxide (NaOH) alkaline neutralization wash.
  4. Sealing Process: Chars stay under continuous inert gas flow until cooling below 100°C before extraction to block environmental contamination.
