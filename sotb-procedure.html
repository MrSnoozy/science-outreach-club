Experimental Synthesis Procedure

Standardized protocols for BC conifer waste conditioning and multi-mode pyrolysis execution.

1. Feedstock Biomass Preparation

To accurately map trace Iron (\(\text{Fe}\)) and Manganese (\(\text{Mn}\)) baselines, processing starts with uniform material control guidelines:

  • Sourcing: Residual raw logging slash waste from British Columbia conifer profiles—primarily a localized matrix mixture of Douglas-fir, Western Hemlock, and Western Red Cedar.
  • Milling & Seeding: Biomass samples are mechanically chipped, oven-dried at \(105^\circ\text{C}\) for 24 hours to eliminate extraneous moisture variables, and sieved into uniform particle cohorts.

2. Pyrolysis Processing Pathways

Our team utilizes two distinct thermodynamic processing arrays to observe how structural carbon matrix changes impact metal reduction behaviors:

Pathway A: Microwave-Assisted Pyrolysis (MWP Series)

Samples are subjected to localized, high-dielectric microwave energy excitation fields. This creates rapid internal thermal hot-spots that force distinct volatile evolution pathways:

  1. Weigh precisely \(\sim10\text{–}50\text{ g}\) of prepared conifer residue into an unreactive, high-temperature quartz reaction vessel.
  2. Seal the chamber and initiate an active inert gas sweep utilizing an ultra-pure Nitrogen (\(\text{N}_2\)) purge at a flow rate of \(1.5\text{ L/min}\) to ensure an oxygen-free processing zone.
  3. Program the microwave field engine to scale progressively up to designated localized temperature holds: **250°C** (for the baseline \(MWP250\) series) or **400°C** (for the intermediate \(MWP400\) series). Maintain peak target limits for 30 minutes.

Pathway B: Conventional Controlled Pyrolysis (CWRB & CSAB Controls)

To evaluate traditional thermal behaviors, samples are processed via standard external heating methods inside a quartz tube furnace environment:

  1. Position the feedstock inside a ceramic boat slider positioned in the center of the furnace hot zone.
  2. Establish an ongoing nitrogen atmosphere configuration to fully stabilize the volatile sweep path.
  3. For CWRB: Ramp the system elements at a fixed rate of \(\sim10^\circ\text{C/min}\) up to a target boundary of **550°C** to track carbothermal interactions near the classic thermodynamic line.
  4. For CSAB: Following initial slow pyrolysis, scale the heating arrays to **900°C** while introducing a secondary fluidizing stream of superheated steam to monitor competitive matrix gasification pathways.

3. Post-Run Preservation Protocols

Because highly porous biochars can undergo rapid surface oxidation when exposed to open air while hot, the nitrogen gas loop remains completely active post-run. Solid samples are cooled below \(60^\circ\text{C}\) before extraction, then sealed in airtight amber vials to preserve their atomic-level metal oxidation states for synchrotron testing.
